How an embroidered patch is applied to apparel and other soft good products is equally important as the patch design itself.

No Backing

This option is ideal for patches that are temporarily being worn on a garment. A patch with no backing is normally affixed to garments using safety pins.

Pvc Backing

This option is ideal for sewn-on patches. PVC backing adds structural support to the patch and does not have iron-on capabilities.

Iron-on Backing

Iron-on backing is the best option if you intend to permanently secure your patch to one single garment.

Peel & Stick Backing

This option eliminates the need to sew-on or iron-on patches to apparel, hats, and bags. The sticky adhesive material on the back of the patch keeps it in place. This option is not permanent.

Hook & Loop Velcro Backing

If your patch is designed to be worn on multiple garments or switched out for other patches worn on the same garment, hook and loop velcro is a popular option. Velcro backing is durable and can be used over and over again without damaging the patch.

TWILL OPTIONS

Twill backing is a smooth polyester fabric that works as the background of your embroidered patch design. Twill is an economical alternative to embroidering the background fills that surround your patch design while creating a perfect contrast with the high quality threads we use. Twill backing is tight and solid and ensures your embroidered image stands out crisp and clear. Arena offers over 30 twill background color options.
